# Case Studies: Emotional Mechanics in Existing Games

## Case 1: Celeste (Madeline & Anxiety)
- **Emotion:** Panic, self-doubt
- **Mechanic:** The player controls Madeline through brutally precise platforming. The difficulty itself embodies her struggle.
- **Externalization:** The "Badeline" manifestation physically blocks paths and chases the player.
- **Lesson:** Difficulty is not punishment—it is the emotional vocabulary.

## Case 2: Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice (Psychosis)
- **Emotion:** Hallucination, unreality
- **Mechanic:** Voices whisper conflicting advice during combat and puzzles. The player cannot trust audio cues.
- **Externalization:** Permadeath warning (later revealed as narrative bluff) creates real anxiety in the player.
- **Lesson:** System-level deception can be an emotional tool when ethically deployed.

## Case 3: Papers, Please (Bureaucratic Oppression)
- **Emotion:** Moral fatigue, complicity
- **Mechanic:** Repetitive document checking. The player must choose between following rules and showing compassion.
- **Externalization:** Time pressure forces hasty decisions that harm fictional people.
- **Lesson:** Repetitive mechanics with moral stakes create emotional weight without complex systems.

## Case 4: Unpacking (Nostalgia / Grief)
- **Emotion:** Life transitions, loss
- **Mechanic:** Placing objects in new spaces. Empty shelves and missing items tell the story.
- **Externalization:** Objects that no longer have a place in the new home.
- **Lesson:** Absence is as mechanically expressive as presence.

## Case 5: The Witness (Confusion / Epiphany)
- **Emotion:** Intellectual overwhelm, then clarity
- **Mechanic:** Line-drawing puzzles that gradually teach a visual language without explicit instruction.
- **Externalization:** The island itself is the puzzle; environment and mechanic fuse.
- **Lesson:** Emotional arc (frustration → breakthrough) can be designed into mechanic progression.
